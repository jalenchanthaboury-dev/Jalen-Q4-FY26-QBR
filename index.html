Leila Howell, HR Manager  ·  May 29, 2026  ·  AE: Bobby Shove
Closed Won
▼
A government-backed institute that helps American manufacturers, universities, and labs work together to build better composite materials, the lightweight, high-strength stuff used in cars, aircraft, defense, and clean energy. They also run workforce training programs to build the next generation of manufacturing talent.
Closed revenue
$100,676
Contract
3 years (HEP)
AE
Bobby Shove
Skill on display
Business acumen and buying committee awareness. I uncovered that hiring was not the primary business case, and translated it into a strategic angle before the AE walked into the demo.
What I uncovered
Leila is a former LinkedIn corporate customer. She already understood Recruiter, Career Pages, and employer branding. This was not an education play.
Leadership would not approve a purchase based on hiring ROI alone. Their north star is securing more partners for workforce development programs, not filling roles faster.
Leila connected Career Pages and employer branding directly to partner ecosystem visibility. Stronger LinkedIn presence = credibility with manufacturers, educators, workforce orgs, and government partners.
Hiring pain still real: manual applicant tracking, 4 roles awaiting approval, new HR generalist starting next week. But this was the secondary business case, not the primary.
Why this deal was different
Most SDRs would have qualified this as a hiring opportunity and handed it off. I saw around the corner on the real buying committee. The executive case was ecosystem growth, not talent acquisition.
Handoff notes to Bobby included a dual-track story: LinkedIn for hiring AND LinkedIn for partner ecosystem visibility. That framing is what made this a 3-year HEP deal, not a transactional single-seat close.

Underwood Engineers
Diane Sciola, HR Manager  ·  May 4, 2026  ·  AE: Ryan Borquez
Qualified
▼
A 40+ year old engineering firm specializing in wastewater, stormwater, and municipal water infrastructure. If you have ever used a public water treatment facility or driven past a stormwater project, Underwood probably designed or built it.
Skill on display
Objection handling and reframing. Diane wanted to push the call to June because she felt they were "too premature." I turned that around on the same call by repositioning myself as a strategic consultant, not a vendor.
The objection
Diane opened the call by saying she was calling to tell me they were not ready. They were still finalizing job descriptions. She wanted to push everything to June.
The reframe
Instead of accepting the pushback, I challenged it directly. Diane had just told me the roles were needed "yesterday" and in the same breath asked to push our conversation to June. I pointed out that contradiction to her.
Reframed it as: "too premature" was the exact reason to talk now, not in June. Waiting until everything is ready delays access to the exact niche candidates they were already struggling to find.
Positioned the call as purely educational. Not to change her process, not to replace her agencies, just to give her the LinkedIn playbook so she has it in her back pocket when hiring ramps.
We landed on mutual agreement that it made more sense to connect sooner and get ahead of the challenges rather than wait. Booked for the following Thursday with Ryan Borquez instead of June. Would not have happened if I had accepted the pushback at face value.
What I uncovered underneath the objection
Roles needed yesterday. 3-4 project managers and technical leaders open right now.
7 staffing agencies working on these roles. 3-4 resumes in a few weeks. Agencies charging 20-25% contingent fees.
Team spread thin. Deadlines at risk. "We lose money when we miss deadlines."
Status
Deal in progress with Ryan Borquez. Regardless of outcome, this call demonstrates that "too premature" is not the end of the conversation. It is often the beginning of one.

What contributed to my successful Q4
⏱️
Ran every qual call as a real 30 minute discovery
Stopped treating quals as a quick surface-level check-in. Made every call a genuine 30 minute discovery so the AE can walk straight into demoing on the next call instead of re-discovering. Shortens the sales cycle before the AE even touches the deal.
🍳
Intentional time with former SDs now in the AE seat
Made a habit of grabbing breakfast or lunch with reps who made the exact jump I am working toward. Asked them directly what it actually took to succeed consistently once they got there, not just how they got promoted.
🎯
Personally invested in better prospect data
Subscribed to an external tool out of my own pocket to get accurate contact data on prospects. One of the biggest unlocks of the quarter. Reaching the right person faster changes everything downstream.
📧
Voicemail plus hyper personalized follow up
Paired every voicemail with a follow up email that made the case for why now, specifically for that company, tied to their talent initiatives and where LinkedIn could optimize what they were already doing.
🔥
Avoided complacency, stayed hungry for the next gap
Never settled into a comfortable rhythm. Always looking for the next competency to strengthen, whether that was objection handling, prospecting efficiency, or solution mapping. Carved out dedicated time to develop each one instead of waiting for it to become a problem.
🎧
Studied AE deals on Gong start to finish
Beyond consistent Hyperbound reps, I watched full AE deal cycles on Gong from the first call to close. Not clips, entire deals. Learning how top AEs sequence a deal, not just how they run a single call.
